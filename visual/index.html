Results

Based on a Artificial Neural Network (ANN) trained using backprogogation and data from 1999 to 2012, the week-over-week stock market can be predicted to rise or fall with 60.6% accuracy. This prediction is more accurate for increases (68%) than for decreases (47%).

About

We constructed a multilayer perceptron (MLP) feedforward neural network that used backprogagation to train the weights of the network for the purpose of predicting the direction of the stock market. In the process, we exploring a wide variety of possibilities both for the data and neural net parameters.

Background & Motivation

Predicting the stock market is certainly a new endeavor. However, past approaches have had shortcomings which we wanted to address, at least in part: past models depended on human intutition for the selection of input data (i.e. what should we put in the network?), and were also nontrivial to implement both in terms of computation power required and the data to be gathered. In this project, we aimed to take a more rigorous approach to data selection which incorporates not only human intuition, but an algorithmic examination of data relevance. By doing so, we were able to find a set of input data that provides reasonable accuracy with relatively little total volume of data. While our work may not be at the scale or scope of larger commercial applications,it does resemble an original application of ANNs for financial data and an advancement in network pruning, a major topic among financial forecasting with ANNs in the scientific literature.

In terms of project motivation, we wanted to work on a project that was both novel and challenging: the computational and data-related aspects of neural networks are difficult and we were able to gain expereince on a practical programmatic level. In addition, we wanted to focus on a project that was relevant and applicable: one of the major questions unaswered by our project is if the neural net we created could serve as a basis for a trading strategy.

Collecting the Data

Before we decided on the specific data we were going to use, we first decided to download important financial metrics and begin exploring to determine how we would approach finding relevant financial data for our neural network. To collect data used the services of Quandl, an API service which allowed us to explore and download relevant financial data.
